To keep weight off, there are a few key principles to focus on: 1. Burn calories. First and foremost, to keep weight off, you want to choose exercises that burn calories efficiently. 2. Higher impact exercises are okay. Since you have a relatively lower weight already, your joints are not stressed as severely by higher impact exercises, so if they burn more calories, go ahead and choose them. 3. Pick something you enjoy. Since the key to keeping weight off is lifelong habits, make sure to pick exercises that you enjoy so that you are motivated to continue exercising throughout your life.

When trying to build muscle while having a high BMI, there are a few key principles to focus on when choosing exercises. 1. Spend some time on calorie-burning exercises, and some time on muscle-building exercises. While there is overlap between these categories, it is important to lose weight while attempting to gain muscle. This is because weighlifting puts significant stress on the joints while losing weight helps take stress off the joints to stay healthy. 2. Pick low impact calorie-burning exercises. Since weightlifting is already stressing the joints, try to pick lower impact calorie-burning exercises that do not stress the joints as severely.
